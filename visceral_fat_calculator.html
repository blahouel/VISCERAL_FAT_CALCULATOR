What is Visceral Fat?

Visceral fat is stored deep inside your abdomen around your internal organs. It's linked to increased health risks like heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and stroke, even in people with normal BMI.

How to Reduce Visceral Fat

Regular aerobic exercise, strength training, reducing refined carbs and sugars, increasing protein and fiber intake, and managing stress can help reduce visceral fat.

Measurement Guide

Accurate measurements are key for a precise calculation.

1. Waist Circumference

Stand straight and breathe out naturally. Place the tape measure midway between the bottom of your ribs and the top of your hip bone (iliac crest). Keep the tape parallel to the floor and snug, but not digging into your skin.

2. Thigh Circumference

Stand with your weight evenly distributed on both feet. Measure around the widest part of your upper thigh, just below the gluteal fold. Ensure the tape is level all the way around.

Exercise Spotlight: MIIT (Medium Intensity Interval Training)

Why MIIT? While High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is popular, Medium Intensity Interval Training (MIIT) is often more sustainable and equally effective for reducing visceral fat. MIIT involves alternating between moderate effort (where you can talk but it's difficult) and active recovery.


MIIT bridges the gap between walking and sprinting. It keeps cortisol levels lower than extreme HIIT while still stimulating fat oxidation and improving metabolic health.
